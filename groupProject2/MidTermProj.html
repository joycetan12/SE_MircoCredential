Stay informed of any changes

Covid-19 spread to 219 countries with over 114000000 plus cases worldwide. — Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)

COVID-19 is caused by a coronavirus called SARS-CoV-2. On March 01, 2020 the first confirmed covid case in the city of New York was recorded. The numbers quickly raised and the City became an epicenter of the pandemic. By April 4th, 2020 12,274 new cases were recorded and a sadly more than approximately 29,000 deaths were reported for the month of. Since then government officials has made large efforts to ensure the safety of all New York Residents by limiting access to places that posed at a risk factor if occupied by its normal number of people e.g. schools, restaurants, churches, bars, parks etc. New York’s Governor developed a phase system in which the state followed to ensure that the covid numbers decreased. New York City seemingly moving through the current phases of reopening but residents must continue to make smart choices and do what’s necessary to protect each other’s health and wellbeing. The phased system has been functioning well and the reopening of New York is happening on a regional basis. According to the states covid information websites; The infection rate is sufficiently low; The health care system has the capacity to absorb a potential resurgence in new cases; Diagnostic testing capacity is sufficiently high to detect and isolate new cases; and Robust contact-tracing capacity is in place to help prevent the spread of the virus. To learn more about New York’s cases and the reopening phases please visit https://forward.ny.gov/
